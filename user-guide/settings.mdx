## Overview

The Settings section allows you to customize your MediaStream account, manage security features, and personalize your experience. Access settings by clicking your profile menu in the sidebar and selecting **Settings**.

## Accessing Settings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open User Menu">
    Click on your profile picture or name in the bottom-left corner of the sidebar.
    
    This opens a dropdown menu with your account options.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Settings">
    Click the **Settings** option (gear icon) in the dropdown menu.
    
    You'll be taken to the Settings page with a sidebar navigation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Profile Settings

### Updating Profile Information

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Profile">
    Click **Profile** in the settings sidebar.
    
    Breadcrumb: "Profile settings"
  </Step>

  <Step title="Edit Your Information">
    Update the following fields:
    
    **Name** (Required)
    - Your full name as displayed in the application
    - Autocomplete: name
    
    **Email Address** (Required)
    - Your account email address
    - Used for login and notifications
    - Autocomplete: username
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save Changes">
    Click the **Save** button to update your profile.
    
    - Button is disabled while processing
    - "Saved." message appears briefly on success
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Email Verification

If your email is unverified, you'll see:

<Note>
**Your email address is unverified.**

Click the link to resend the verification email. You'll receive a confirmation message when the email is sent.
</Note>

Once you click to resend:
- A new verification link is sent to your email
- Green confirmation text appears: "A new verification link has been sent to your email address"
- Check your inbox and click the verification link

## Password Settings

Keep your account secure by regularly updating your password.

### Changing Your Password

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Password Settings">
    Click **Password** in the settings sidebar.
    
    Breadcrumb: "Password settings"
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Password Information">
    Fill in all three required fields:
    
    **Current Password** (Required)
    - Your existing password for verification
    - Autocomplete: current-password
    
    **New Password** (Required)
    - Your new password
    - Should be long and random for security
    - Autocomplete: new-password
    
    **Confirm Password** (Required)
    - Re-enter your new password to confirm
    - Must match the new password exactly
    - Autocomplete: new-password
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save New Password">
    Click **Save password** to update.
    
    - Button disabled during processing
    - "Saved." confirmation appears on success
    - Form fields clear automatically
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Make sure to use a strong password that includes a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.
</Warning>

## Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Add an extra layer of security to your account with two-factor authentication.

### Understanding 2FA

Two-factor authentication requires two forms of verification:
1. Your password (something you know)
2. A time-based code from your phone (something you have)

### Enabling Two-Factor Authentication

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to 2FA Settings">
    Click **Two-Factor Auth** in the settings sidebar.
    
    You'll see a badge showing current status: **Disabled** (red) or **Enabled** (default).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enable 2FA">
    If 2FA is currently disabled:
    
    1. Read the information about how 2FA works
    2. Click the **Enable 2FA** button (shield-check icon)
    3. Wait for the setup modal to appear
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Up Authenticator App">
    A modal will guide you through setup:
    
    1. Install a TOTP-supported app on your phone (like Google Authenticator, Authy, or Microsoft Authenticator)
    2. Scan the QR code displayed in the modal
    3. Enter the 6-digit code from your authenticator app
    4. Confirm to complete setup
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save Recovery Codes">
    **Important**: Save your recovery codes in a secure location.
    
    - Recovery codes allow access if you lose your phone
    - Each code can only be used once
    - Store them securely offline
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
If you exit the setup process before confirming, you can click **Continue Setup** to resume where you left off.
</Tip>

### Managing 2FA When Enabled

Once 2FA is enabled, you can:

**View Recovery Codes**
- Access your saved recovery codes
- Generate new codes if needed
- Download or print for safekeeping

**Disable 2FA**
- Click the red **Disable 2FA** button (shield-ban icon)
- Confirm your decision
- 2FA will be removed from your account

<Warning>
Disabling 2FA reduces your account security. Only disable if absolutely necessary.
</Warning>

### Using 2FA During Login

Once enabled, the login process changes:

1. Enter your email and password as usual
2. You'll be prompted for a 2FA code
3. Open your authenticator app
4. Enter the current 6-digit code
5. Click verify to complete login

<Accordion title="Lost access to authenticator app?">
  If you lose access to your authenticator app:
  
  1. Use one of your recovery codes to log in
  2. Go to 2FA settings and disable 2FA
  3. Set up 2FA again with a new authenticator app
  4. Save your new recovery codes
  
  If you've lost both your authenticator app and recovery codes, contact support for account recovery assistance.
</Accordion>

## Common Settings Tasks

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I change my display name?">
    Go to Profile settings, update the **Name** field, and click Save. Your new name will appear immediately throughout the application.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="I forgot my password">
    If you're logged out, use the "Forgot Password" link on the login page. If you're logged in, go to Password settings and change it using your current password.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can I use the same email for multiple accounts?">
    No, each account requires a unique email address. You'll need different email addresses for separate accounts.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="How do I know if 2FA is working?">
    After enabling 2FA, log out and log back in. You should be prompted for a 6-digit code from your authenticator app.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What if I change my email?">
    When you change your email in Profile settings, you'll need to verify the new address before it becomes active.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
